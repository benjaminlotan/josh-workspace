§ 02 / Manifesto

Eight rules for the workshop.

Index
01

A photograph is an object.

We do not make screens. We make paper, ink, glass, metal, brass, and cloth. Every product in this shop is a thing your hands will recognize before your eyes do.

PRINCIPLE
02

Show the grid.

Structure is not embarrassing. The grid is how the work stays honest. We show our columns, our margins, our specifications, and our prices.

METHOD
03

Numbers are a love language.

200 gsm. 1/250s at f/8. ISO 100. Fifteen years. Ten humans. We talk about our work the way photographers talk about their tools — exactly.

VOICE
04

No decoration without function.

Every rule, every tag, every colophon line earns its place by holding information. If it does not, we cut it. The discipline is the design.

RESTRAINT
05

Two greys, one green, one accent.

The whole house operates from a seven-color palette. The work of a print shop is to make photographs sing — the room around them stays quiet.

PALETTE
06

Treat the catalog like a museum.

Each product gets a number, a specification, a price, and a fair amount of light. Customers are not browsers; they are visitors. We label everything.

CATALOG
07

Hairlines, never shadows.

Depth is achieved with rules and adjacencies, not with falsified light. One pixel of true ink will out-perform any blur.

SURFACE
08

Built in Seattle, made for the world.

47.6062° N, 122.3321° W. We ship from a warehouse you can see from the freeway. International by attitude, Pacific Northwest by address.

We treat each print like a small piece of architecture — chosen paper, exact margins, considered binding, signed boxes. The catalog is short on purpose. We sell eight things and we make all of them well.

DM Sans is the workhorse of the system. It carries body copy, navigation, captions, prices, and product names. It is humanist enough to feel made by a person, geometric enough to disappear into the grid.
