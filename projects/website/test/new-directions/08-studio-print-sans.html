Plates I–VII · Six colours, one rule

The palette.

Seven colours pulled from a paper mill at golden hour. Ink for the type, paper and vellum for the substrate, a quiet forest and its companion moss for the brand voice, a single burnt accent for what matters most, and a hairline rule the colour of dried wheat. Nothing more is needed.

#1B1B1B

Primary background. A warm cream the colour of an unread first edition.

Alternating section ground. A richer aged tone for breathing room and contrast.

Authoritative deep green. Primary actions, marks, and quiet authority.

Body · DM Sans · 17 / 28

DM Sans carries the reading. It is a low-contrast geometric sans by Colophon Foundry and Indian Type Foundry, drawn to feel both contemporary and warm in extended text. Set at seventeen pixels with a sixty-two percent line-height, it reads like a well-printed paperback — the same role Manrope held in the original Studio Print, here with a slightly softer grotesk character.

The face supports three weights in the system — four hundred for running text, five hundred for emphasis and labels, and seven hundred for the rare moment when emphasis must speak louder. Weight, never colour, never tracking, is how this body type carries voice.

Colophon.

This document was set in Bricolage Grotesque, a contemporary variable grotesk by Mathieu Triay, paired with DM Sans for the reading and IBM Plex Mono for the metadata. It is a reskin of Studio Print — the same classical bookmaking structure, restyled with a sans-serif lead. Composed on the occasion of Social Print Studio's sixteenth year of operation.
