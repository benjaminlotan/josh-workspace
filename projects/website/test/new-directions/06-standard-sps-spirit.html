opening  statement

we are a small print studio that makes photographic objects you would actually keep. tinybooks. layflat albums. gallery frames. the kind of printed thing that survives a move, outlives a phone, and earns a place on a shelf.

since 2010 we have believed in photos as a force for good — and we have built our entire practice around that single, stubborn idea.

section  02 / 06
craft

how it is made.

every object in our catalog passes through the same small studio. paper is chosen first, press settings second, binding third, finishing last. we work the way print shops worked before software told them not to. nothing here is automated end-to-end — people are still in the loop, and the loop is the point.

section  04 / 06
studio

a small practice.

started in brooklyn in 2010 by a two-person team. now a ten-person studio working out of a single building in seattle. the company has stayed small on purpose — small enough that the people who answer the phone are also the people who run the press.

a brooklyn beginning.

started in 2010 in a small print shop in brooklyn making 4×4 prints from instagram. that scrappy beginning still shapes how we work: small batch, hand-finished, and built around the photo, not the platform.

a seattle present.

now operating from a studio in seattle, with a long-running customer service team that answers the phone in the morning and walks an order through the press in the afternoon.

a stubborn principle.

we believe a printed photograph is the most under-used object in the modern home. our entire job is to put more of them in more rooms, in formats that can survive the next twenty years.
