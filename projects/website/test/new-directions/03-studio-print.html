Chapter I
Plates I–VII · Six colours, one rule

The palette.

Seven colours pulled from a paper mill at golden hour. Ink for the type, paper and vellum for the substrate, a quiet forest and its companion moss for the brand voice, a single burnt accent for what matters most, and a hairline rule the colour of dried wheat. Nothing more is needed.

Ink
#1B1B1B

Body type, primary surface on dark ground. The voice of the page.

Paper
#F8F4EC

Primary background. A warm cream the colour of an unread first edition.

Vellum
#EBE3D2

Alternating section ground. A richer aged tone for breathing room and contrast.

Forest
#2C3D33

Authoritative deep green. Primary actions, marks, and quiet authority.

Moss
#5C7261

Secondary green. Hovers, soft fields, the in-between voice.

Burnt
#A0432B

A single burnt sienna for what truly matters. Used as rarely as a colophon mark.

Rule
#C8BFA8

Hairline borders, dividers, the thin lines that make the architecture visible without raising its voice.

Body · Manrope · 17 / 28

Manrope carries the reading. It is a modern humanist sans designed by Mikhail Sharanda, slightly geometric in the bones but warmed by the open apertures and the generous x-height. Set at seventeen pixels with a sixty-two percent line-height, it reads like a well-printed paperback.

The face supports four weights in the system — three hundred for delicate notation, four hundred for running text, five hundred for emphasis and labels, and seven hundred for the rare moment when emphasis must shout. Italics are reserved for titles, foreign words, and the names of works.

Colophon.

This document was set in Bodoni Moda, a contemporary revival of Giambattista Bodoni's late-eighteenth-century Didone, paired with Manrope for the reading and Space Mono for the metadata.
