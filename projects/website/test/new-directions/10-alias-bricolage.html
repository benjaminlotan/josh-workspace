02/Identity

A studio identity, not a storefront skin.

01Studio of record
02Catalog & editions
03Print & bind
04Distribution

For fifteen years we've been a quiet printer. We sent the boxes, we kept our heads down, and we let the photographs do the talking. That part isn't changing. What changes is the room around them.

Alias proposes that Social Print Studio behave like the contemporary art studio it has always been in private — a working operation with a point of view, a catalog, an archive, and an opinion about what a printed photograph is for. The system is rigorous without being cold. There is one accent color, one display face, one body face, one mono. The grid is twelve columns. The corners are sharp.

Everything else is in service of the picture.

03/Palette

Seven colors,
one of them sharp.

The palette holds itself in restraint so that the rust accent — used once per section — actually does its job. Bone replaces cream. Forest replaces silken pine. Rust replaces terracotta. Everything else moves out of the way.

Primary surface inversion. Body type, rules, primary buttons. Used as anchor.

04/Typography

Three families.
One voice.

Bricolage Grotesque carries display moments — humanist, slightly quirky, warm at scale. Plus Jakarta Sans handles every paragraph and label. JetBrains Mono handles metadata and the small technical voice that makes a studio feel like a studio.

An identity system is a set of quiet decisions made loud enough that nobody on the team has to make them again.

Plus Jakarta Sans is the workhorse: paragraphs, ledes, captions, button labels, navigation, form fields, and the supporting voice of the studio. It is geometric enough to feel modern and humanist enough to read at small sizes. Numerals are tabular. Italics carry editorial moments without needing a second face.

06/System

Components, set in
their working room.

Buttons are sharp rectangles. The primary CTA carries a single rust underline beneath the label — the only ornament in the system. Tags are hairline-bordered mono. Form fields strip the box and lean on a baseline rule.

Buttons · primary & secondary 06.A

Primary CTAs use the rust underline variant — exactly one per section. Secondary buttons fill ink. Ghost buttons invert on hover. No rounded corners, no drop shadows, no exceptions.
