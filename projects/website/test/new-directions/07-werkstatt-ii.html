§ 01 / Identity Werkstatt II

A workshop for photographs made into objects.

Social Print Studio is a fifteen-year-old print workshop in Seattle. Photographs into books, frames, prints, and other things you keep.

§ 02 / Manifesto

Six rules for the workshop.

01

A photograph is an object.

Paper, ink, glass, metal, brass, cloth. We make things your hands recognize before your eyes do.

02

Numbers are a love language.

200 gsm. 1/250 at f/8. Fifteen years. Ten humans. We talk about our work the way photographers talk about their tools.

03

No decoration without function.

Every rule, every label, every line earns its place by holding information. If it doesn't, we cut it.

04

The room around the photograph stays quiet.

Two greys, one green, one warm accent. The whole house operates from a seven-color palette so the work can sing.

05

Hairlines, never shadows.

Depth is achieved with rules and adjacencies, not with falsified light. One pixel of true ink out-performs any blur.

06

Built in Seattle, made for the world.

We ship from a warehouse you can see from the freeway. International by attitude, Pacific Northwest by address.

Social Print Studio has been printing photographs for fifteen years from a warehouse in Seattle. We treat each print like a small piece of architecture — chosen paper, exact margins, considered binding, signed boxes. The catalog is short on purpose. We sell eight things and we make all of them well.

DM Sans is the workhorse of the system. It carries body copy, navigation, captions, prices, and product names. Humanist enough to feel made by a person, geometric enough to disappear into the grid.
