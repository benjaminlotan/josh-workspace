# Recipe: Regular Check-In

How Josh runs a regular check-in with Ben or George, or both of them together. This is the light-touch rhythm (weekly or biweekly) that keeps things from slipping through cracks. Different from a strategic-brainstorm, which is a deep dive on a specific topic.

## When to Use
- Scheduled weekly or biweekly check-in
- Ben says "let's catch up" or "what's on your mind"
- Josh has accumulated enough context updates that a sync is needed

## Before the Check-In

### 1. Read memory
- Read `context.md` for current state
- Skim `session-log.md` for last session's action items and open threads
- Check `sps-knowledge.md` for any sections that feel thin or outdated — note questions to ask Ben

### 2. Check in with Marcus
- Read `../project-manager/master-overview.md` for current project status
- Note anything that seems off, stalled, or strategically relevant

### 3. Prepare an agenda
Come to the check-in with:
- Status on open action items from last time (who owes what)
- 1-2 things Josh has been thinking about or wants to raise
- Questions about gaps in Josh's knowledge of the business
- Any signals Josh has been watching that have developed

## During the Check-In

### 4. Open
"Here's what I've been tracking since last time..." — brief status on what's moved, what hasn't, what's changed.

### 5. Follow-up accountability
Go through open action items. For each:
- Done? Great, close it.
- Not done? Why? Is it still the right priority? New deadline?
- Blocked? What's in the way? Can Josh help?

### 6. Surface what matters
Raise the 1-2 things Josh has been thinking about. These might be:
- A strategic question: "I've been thinking about X. Here's where I'm landing..."
- A concern: "I noticed Y happening. Is that intentional?"
- An opportunity: "Have we thought about Z?"
- A knowledge gap: "I realize I don't know enough about [area]. Can we spend 5 minutes on it?"

### 7. Ben's turn
Ask: "What's on your mind? Anything keeping you up?"
Listen. This is often where the most important things surface.

### 8. Prioritize
End with: "So for the next [week/two weeks], the top priorities are..." — confirm alignment on what matters most.

## After the Check-In

### 9. Update memory
This is mandatory. After every check-in:
1. Append a session entry to `session-log.md`
2. Update `context.md` — current priorities, new action items, resolved threads
3. Update `sps-knowledge.md` with anything new learned about the business
4. Update `people.md` if anything was discussed about team members or contacts
5. Update `profile.md` if Josh noticed something new about how Ben thinks or operates

### 10. Flag cross-agent items
If anything from the check-in is relevant to another agent:
- Project status change → Marcus should know
- Personal topic surfaced → might be relevant for Simon or Barbara
- Design or creative question → creative partner territory

Note these in context.md under Follow-Up Items with the relevant agent tagged.
